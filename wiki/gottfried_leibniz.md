# Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz

**Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz** (1646–1716) was a German mathematician, philosopher, and polymath. He is one of the most important inventors of calculus.

## Contributions to Calculus

- **Notation**: Leibniz introduced the widely used dx/dy notation for the [[derivative]] and the integral symbol \(\int\). His notation was highly intuitive and became the standard worldwide.
- **Rules of Calculus**: Leibniz developed the product rule and chain rule independently (see [[differentiation_rules]]).
- **Co-invention Controversy**: Leibniz published his work on calculus in 1684, before [[isaac_newton]] published his, leading to a long-running dispute over priority. Modern history credits both men with independent discovery.
