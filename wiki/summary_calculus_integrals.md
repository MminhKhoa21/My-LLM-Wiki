# Summary: Calculus Integrals Study Guide

This document summarizes the core topics from the [Calculus Integrals Study Guide](../raw/calculus_integrals.txt) raw source.

## Key Takeaways

- **The Integral**: An integral represents the area under a curve. A definite integral computes signed area between boundaries; an indefinite integral represents the family of antiderivatives.
- **Inverse Operation**: The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus links integration and differentiation, proving that integration is the inverse of finding a [[derivative]].
- **Notation**: The standard integration symbol \(\int\) was introduced by [[gottfried_leibniz]].
- **Inventors**: Both [[isaac_newton]] and [[gottfried_leibniz]] co-developed integration methodologies.
